# Trump-Iran Update: March 29, 2026

As of Sunday, March 29, 2026, the relationship between President Donald Trump and Iran is at a critical juncture, characterized by intense military pressure and a recently extended "strike pause."

## 1. Strike Pause Extended to April 6
On March 26, 2026, President Trump announced an extension of the current pause on military strikes against Iranian energy infrastructure. 
*   **New Deadline:** The pause is set to last until Monday, April 6, 2026, at 8:00 P.M. ET. 
*   **The Goal:** Trump stated the extension was requested by the Iranian government to facilitate ongoing negotiations for a new peace deal. 

## 2. Operation Epic Fury & Military Progress
The U.S. and Israel have been engaged in Operation Epic Fury since late February 2026, a series of airstrikes aimed at degrading Iran's military capabilities.
*   **Current Standing:** The Pentagon reports that approximately 90% of Iran’s missile launchers have been neutralized.
*   **Strait of Hormuz:** A major point of contention remains the Strait of Hormuz, which is under a partial blockade by Iran. Reopening the Strait is a non-negotiable condition for the U.S. to maintain the strike pause.

## 3. The 15-Point Peace Proposal
The Trump administration, through special envoy Steve Witkoff, has presented a 15-point peace proposal to Tehran. 
*   **Trump's Stance:** He has publicly demanded "unconditional surrender" and a change in leadership to a more "acceptable" regime. He claims talks are "going very well," though Tehran has officially dismissed the proposal as "fake news" and a manipulation of oil markets.
*   **Oil as Leverage:** Trump has maintained that targeting or seizing Iranian oil assets (specifically Kharg Island) remains a "viable option" if a deal is not reached.

## 4. Economic and Global Impact
*   **Oil Prices:** The instability in the Persian Gulf and the blockade of the Strait of Hormuz have caused global oil prices to surge, leading to widespread market volatility.
*   **International Reaction:** While the U.S. continues its "maximum pressure" campaign, G7 allies have expressed concern over strikes on civilian-linked infrastructure and the potential for a full-scale ground war, though the U.S. State Department currently maintains they hope to avoid a ground invasion.

In summary, the next week is pivotal, with the April 6 deadline serving as a final countdown for Iran to accept the terms of the U.S. peace proposal before military operations potentially resume or escalate.
